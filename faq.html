Why does the date for Easter change every year?

expand_more
Easter changes every year because it follows the lunar calendar. It's based on the first Sunday after the first full moon that comes after the Spring Equinox (around March 21st). So depending on the moon, Easter can be anywhere between March 22nd and April 25th!

expand_more
The earliest Easter can ever happen is March 22nd, and the latest it can fall is April 25th. This is all because of how Easter's date is tied to the moon and the equinox!

Is Easter the same date worldwide?

expand_more
Not exactly! Most people celebrate Easter on the same day, but some churches, like those following the Eastern Orthodox calendar, might have a different date, known as Orthodox Easter. Their Easter is based on a slightly different formula tied to the Julian calendar, not the Gregorian one most of us use.

What is Orthodox Easter?

expand_more
Orthodox Easter is a Christian holiday celebrated by Orthodox Christian churches, which includes the Eastern Orthodox Church and Oriental Orthodox churches. It usually falls on a different date than Western Christian Easter because the Orthodox Church uses the Julian calendar instead of the Gregorian calendar. Orthodox Easter celebrates the resurrection of Jesus Christ, just like Western Easter, but it's observed with different traditions, dates, and customs.

Why does the font of the tracker look weird?

expand_more
The tracker uses Google Fonts API to load the fonts, which gives the site its clean and modern look. However, if you notice any weird fonts or the text seems off, it could be due to your browser or network blocking fonts.googleapis.com. To fix this, make sure that the domain is unblocked in your browser or network settings. If you use a DNS blocker like Pi-hole, make sure this service isn't blocking font resources either. If it isn't blocked, try refreshing the page. Once that's fixed, the fonts should load correctly!
